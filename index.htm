About Me

While going through high school, student Harish Bommakanti from Rouse High School in Leander, Texas felt frustrated whenever he needed to search extensively for resources to supplement his school education in order to fully understand a concept. Harish wished there could be an all inclusive "student corner" where one would find all things academic, from study guides for his upcoming unit test to SAT prep sites. Harish created this site in order to aid future high schoolers in going through the challenging high school process.

Math

Math can be difficult for many high schoolers because of its complexity and the practice that is needed to develop mastery of the subject. Here are few resources to help with most high school math courses.

Common high school mathematics courses in order:

Pre-Algebra, Algebra 1, Pre-AP/On Level Geometry, Pre-AP/On Level Algebra 2, On-Ramps/Pre-AP/On Level Pre-Calculus, AP/On Level Statistics*, AP Calculus AB*, AP Calculus BC*

These math courses aim to build on the fundamental algabraic concepts needed in college mathematics. There are plenty of sites that walk through problems step by step in order to educate students and help them understand the otherwise complex math topics for high school students.

  • JoAnn's School (Youtube Channel)
    • These videos cover math topics straight from the classroom from elementary school to high school math.
  • Khan Academy
    • Khan Academy offers a wide vareity of math tutoring covering virtually all mathematics courses possible, from as early as 1st grade math to complex courses like Multivariable Calculus. Content is usually taught through videos that walk one through a problem or problem type. A short quiz is then given to confirm understanding of the subject. This site is especially fun as it gives out badges and acheivements for certain goals, providing incentive to study mathematics!
  • Purplemath
    • Purplemath is a bank of informal lessons teaching students the more fundamental lessons of mathematics, like seen in Pre-Algebra, Algebra 1, and Algebra 2. It starts to teach trignometry, which is covered in Pre-Calculus, but it doesn't extend its lessons as far as Khan Acaemy does. Nevertheless, it is a great resource for practice problems and thoroughly understanding the various types of problems seen in high school.
  • Math is Fun
    • This site is one of the most informal math sites because it focuses on fun scenarios, math puzzles, and interactives to help one understand a problem. It stretches from basic algebra and geometry to calculus, statistics, and even physics. This site is great if you're looking for a simple explanation for any type of math problem.
  • Dan Shuster's AP Statistics Notes
    • These chapter notes published by Dan Shuster, a teacher at Royal High School, do a superb job at summarizing each chapter in AP Statistics and serve as an excellent review for any AP Statistics unit test.
  • Other Resources
    • The following resources serve as excellent video/lesson supplements for advanced mathematics courses such as AP Calculus BC and beyond. They also include calculators which can walk through problems step-by-step.
      • Professor Leonard's Youtube Channel
      • Numberphile: Fun, provoking math concepts (Youtube Channel)
      • 3Blue1Brown (Youtube Channel)
      • Step-by-step Calculator: Symbolab
      • Wolphram Alpha Calculator
* These courses may not follow the course order listed or may be optional.

Science

Science courses are varied and aim to teach students the fundamentals of basic science subjects, focusing mostly on biology, chemistry, and physics. Use any combination of the following resources to help you excel in the courses listed and beyond.

Common high school science courses in order:

Pre-AP/On Level Biology, Pre-AP/On Level Chemistry, Pre-AP/On Level Physics, AP Biology*, AP Chemistry*, AP Physics 1/2*, AP Physics C Electricity/Magnetism*, AP Environmental Science*, Earth and Space Science*

The following resources will mainly cover biology, chemistry, and physics, but can be used for other science courses as many concepts from these courses overlap, as seen in biology and chemistry.

  • Amoeba Sisters
    • Through fun and quirky videos, the Amoeba Sisters videos help to understand fundamental concepts in biology, as seen in On Level and Pre-AP Biology.
  • Bozeman Science
    • Bozeman Science videos help to understand difficult concepts in the AP Biology and Anatomy courses.
  • Crash Course Youtube Videos
    • The Crash Course series on Youtube offers playlists not only for science, but for history, english, psychology, and even mathematics. Although somewhat fast paced, these playlists go through potentially challenging areas in a topic in science, such as Aqueous Equilibrium in Chemistry. These videos are produced with a generous dose of humor that helps to 'diffuse' science concepts.
  • The Physics Classroom
    • The Physics Classroom is an excellent resource for students in high school physics courses. It includes interactives and thorough lessons on physics concepts varied from kinematics, vectors, and electricity. Aside from lessons, this site includes question banks and free apps that can supplement the inforation from a traditional high school physics course.
  • Khan Academy
    • Khan Academy offers a wide vareity of science tutoring covering virtually all high school science courses possible, from common courses such as high school physics to complex lessons dealing with college curricula, such as electrical engineering. Content is usually taught through videos that walk one through a problem or problem type. A short quiz is then given to confirm understanding of the subject. This site is especially fun as it gives out badges and acheivements for certain goals, providing incentive to study science!
  • Barron's
    • Barron's prep books for Advanced Placement (AP) courses may cost money but they are nevertheless a superb resource for science preparation. Through intense and easy-to-follow lessons, practice tests, and online resources, the Barron's guides are ample preparation for any high school science course.
  • Other Resources
    • The following resources serve as excellent video/lesson supplements for advanced science courses such as AP Physics C Electricity/Magnetism and 'just for fun' high school science videos.
      • Michael Van Biezen's Youtube Channel (Physics and Engineering)
      • Tyler Dewitt's Youtube Channel (Chemistry)
      • The Feynman Lectures on Physics
      • Brilliant (Biology, Chemistry, and Physics)
      • 60 Symbols (Youtube Channel)
* These courses may not follow the course order listed or may be optional.

History

Social studies courses do not usually have conceptually hard curricula but require a lot of studying and memorization in order to understand what is going on in any given time period. Use these resources to ease this process.

Common high school history courses in order:

AP Human Geography/On Level Geography, AP/On Level World History, AP European History*, AP/ On Level United States History, AP/On Level Government*, AP/On Level Economics*

The following resources will help high school students in understanding historical trends, main ideas for a certain time periods, or general vocabulary or concepts.

  • Crash Course Youtube Channel
    • John Greene uses clever comedy and historical allusions to illustrate World History, United States History, and Government and Political Science.
  • AP Worldipedia
    • This site thoroughly covers the key concepts specified by Collegeboard for the AP World History curriculum, but it can also be used for on level world history. This resource helps to understand key historical trends and themes in a clear and detailed manner.
  • AP Study Notes
    • The site, AP Study Notes, offers chapter outlines, vocabulary help, and key documents compiled from resources such as Barron's to supplement preparation for AP courses. This information can be adapted to On Level history courses.
      • AP World History Chapter Notes
      • AP United States History Chapter Notes
      • AP European History Chapter Notes
      • AP Government Notes
      • AP Microeconomics Notes
  • Barron's
    • Barron's prep books for Advanced Placement (AP) courses may cost money but they are nevertheless a superb resource for history preparation. Through intense and easy-to-follow lessons, practice tests, and online resources, the Barron's guides are ample preparation for any high school history course.
  • Ethel Wood Books
    • Like books in the Barron's Test Preparation series, books written by Ethel Woods serve as excellent study guides for all AP history courses, including AP Human Geography, AP World History, and AP Government. PDFs of the books can be viewed online or the books can be purchased. The content can be adapted to regular history courses.
* These courses may not follow the course order listed or may be optional.

English

High school english courses can be difficult for a lot of students due to the complexity of the passages being given and the idea of analsis versus summary as seen in advanced english courses. Use these courses to help understand what is needed to excel in high school english courses.

Common high school english courses in order:

Pre-AP/Pre-AP Quest/On Level English 1, Pre-AP/Pre-AP Quest/On Level English 2, On Level English 3/AP English Language and Composition, On Level English 4/AP English Literature, AP Seminar*, AP Research*

These courses aim to prepare high school students for college-level english. Use the following resources to help master the said courses.

  • Crash Course Youtube Channel
    • In the Crash Course Literature Series, John Greene explains key rhetorical and analytical strategies needed to fully digest a novel or piece. These can be adapted to any of the english courses.
  • AP Study Notes: AP English
    • AP Study Notes provides sample essays, rhetorical terms, and extra essay tips to help prepare for the AP English Language and Composition/ AP Literature courses. The information found on this resource can be adapted to all high school english courses, however.
  • Thesaurus.com
    • Although not technically a resource, it always helps to keep this synonym search engine out while working on english essays and assignments to avoid simple words like "big."
      • This resource can be used for any high school english course.
  • Transition Words
    • Like Thesaurus.com, this site provides a wide vareity of transition words while writing for an english assignment or essay. Using transition words adds to the structure and flow of an essay.
      • This resource can be used for any high school english course;
  • Syntax Terms
    • Syntax refers to the struture of a written piece, and how certain patterns or techniques add a deeper meaning to the writing. Use these syntax terms to become more familiar with how you can both recognize syntax terms in others' writing and provide a more complex meaning to your own writing. This become more useful in AP Language and Composition/On Level English 3 and beyond.
  • Tone words
    • The addition of tone words in any essay or assignment adds precision and color to your paper. Use this site for a broad range of verbs and nouns to polish your writing.
      • This resource can be used for any high school english course.
* These courses may not follow the course order listed or may be optional.
