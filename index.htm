About

While going through high school, student Harish Bommakanti from Rouse High School in Leander, Texas felt frustrated whenever he needed to search extensively for resources to supplement his school education in order to fully understand a concept, whether it be in history or physics. Harish wished there could be an all inclusive "student corner" where one would find all things academic, from study guides for his upcoming unit test to SAT prep sites. Harish created this site to be the "student corner" for future high schoolers.

Math

Math can be difficult for many high schoolers because of its complexity and the amount of practice that is needed to develop mastery of the subject. Here are few resources to help with most high school math courses.

Common high school mathematics courses in order:

Pre-Algebra, Algebra 1, Pre-AP/On Level Geometry, Pre-AP/On Level Algebra 2, On-Ramps/Pre-AP/On Level Pre-Calculus, AP/On Level Statistics*, AP Calculus AB*, AP Calculus BC*

These math courses aim to build on the fundamental algabraic concepts needed in college mathematics. There are plenty of sites that walk through problems step by step in order to educate students and help them understand the otherwise complex math topics for high school students.

  • JoAnn's School (Youtube Channel)
    • These videos cover basic math topics straight from the classroom from elementary school to Geometry.
  • Khan Academy
    • Khan Academy offers a wide vareity of math tutoring covering virtually all mathematics courses possible, from as early as 1st grade math to complex courses like Multivariable Calculus. Content is usually taught through videos that walk one through a problem or problem type. A short quiz is then given to confirm understanding of the subject. This site is especially fun as it gives out badges and acheivements for certain goals, providing incentive to study mathematics!
  • Purplemath
    • Purplemath is a bank of informal lessons teaching students the more fundamental lessons of mathematics, like seen in Pre-Algebra, Algebra 1, and Algebra 2. It starts to teach trignometry, which is covered in Pre-Calculus, but it doesn't extend its lessons as far as Khan Acaemy does. Nevertheless, it is a great resource for practice problems and thoroughly understanding the various types of problems seen in high school.
  • Math is Fun
    • This site is one of the most informal math sites because it focuses on fun scenarios, math puzzles, and interactives to help one understand a concept. It stretches from basic algebra and geometry to calculus, statistics, and even physics. This site is great if you're looking for a simple explanation for any type of math problem.
  • Dan Shuster's AP Statistics Notes
    • These chapter notes published by Dan Shuster, a teacher at Royal High School, do a superb job at summarizing each chapter in AP Statistics and serve as an excellent review for any AP Statistics unit test.
  • Other Resources
    • The following resources serve as excellent video/lesson supplements for advanced mathematics courses such as AP Calculus and beyond. They also include calculators which can walk through problems step-by-step.
      • Professor Leonard's Youtube Channel
      • Numberphile: Fun, provoking math concepts (Youtube Channel)
      • 3Blue1Brown (Youtube Channel)
      • Step-by-step Calculator: Symbolab
      • Wolphram Alpha Calculator
* These courses may not follow the course order listed or may be optional.

Science

Science courses are varied and aim to teach students the fundamentals of basic science subjects, focusing mostly on biology, chemistry, and physics. Use any combination of the following resources to help you excel in the courses listed and beyond.

Common high school science courses in order:

Pre-AP/On Level Biology, Pre-AP/On Level Chemistry, Pre-AP/On Level Physics, AP Biology*, AP Chemistry*, AP Physics 1/2*, AP Physics C Electricity/Magnetism*, AP Environmental Science*, Earth and Space Science*

The following resources will mainly cover biology, chemistry, and physics, but can be used for other science courses as many concepts from these courses overlap, as seen in biology and chemistry.

  • Amoeba Sisters
    • Through fun and quirky videos, the Amoeba Sisters videos help to understand fundamental concepts in biology, as seen in On Level and Pre-AP Biology.
  • Bozeman Science
    • Bozeman Science videos help to understand both fundamental and difficult concepts in advanced courses such as AP Biology, AP Chemistry, AP Physics 1/2, and AP Environmental Science.
  • Crash Course Youtube Videos
    • The Crash Course series on Youtube offers playlists not only for science, but for history, english, psychology, and even mathematics. Although somewhat fast paced, these playlists go through potentially challenging areas in a science topics, such as Aqueous Equilibrium in Chemistry. These videos are produced with a generous dose of humor that helps to 'diffuse' science concepts.
  • The Physics Classroom
    • The Physics Classroom is an excellent resource for students in high school physics courses. It includes interactives and thorough lessons on physics concepts varied from kinematics, vectors, and electricity. Aside from lessons, this site includes question banks and free apps that can supplement the inforation from a traditional high school physics course.
  • Khan Academy
    • Khan Academy offers a wide vareity of science tutoring covering virtually all high school science courses possible, from common courses such as high school physics to complex lessons dealing with college curricula, such as electrical engineering. Content is usually taught through videos that walk one through a problem or problem type. A short quiz is then given to confirm understanding of the subject. This site is especially fun as it gives out badges and acheivements for certain goals, providing incentive to study science!
  • Barron's
    • Barron's prep books for Advanced Placement (AP) courses may cost money but they are nevertheless a superb resource for science preparation. Through intense and easy-to-follow lessons, practice tests, and online resources, the Barron's guides are ample preparation for any high school science course.
  • Other Resources
    • The following resources serve as excellent video/lesson supplements for advanced science courses such as AP Physics C Electricity/Magnetism and 'just for fun' high school science videos.
      • Michael Van Biezen's Youtube Channel (Physics and Engineering)
      • Tyler Dewitt's Youtube Channel (Chemistry)
      • The Feynman Lectures on Physics
      • Brilliant (Biology, Chemistry, and Physics)
      • 60 Symbols (Youtube Channel)

History

AP Human Geography/On Level Geography, AP/On Level World History, AP European History*, AP/ On Level United States History, AP/On Level Government*, AP Macroeconomics/Microeconomics or On Level Economics*

The following resources will help high school students in understanding historical trends, main ideas for a certain time periods, or general vocabulary or concepts.

  • AP Worldipedia
    • This site thoroughly covers the key concepts specified by Collegeboard for the AP World History curriculum, but it can also be used for on level world history. This resource helps to understand key historical trends and themes in a clear and detailed manner.
  • AP Study Notes
    • The site offers chapter outlines, vocabulary help, and key documents compiled from resources such as Barron's to supplement preparation for AP courses. This information can be adapted to On Level history courses.
      • AP World History Chapter Notes
      • AP United States History Chapter Notes
      • AP European History Chapter Notes
      • AP Government Notes
      • AP Microeconomics/Macroeconomics Notes
  • Barron's
    • Barron's prep books for Advanced Placement (AP) courses may cost money but they are nevertheless a superb resource for history preparation. Through intense and easy-to-follow lessons, practice tests, and online resources, the Barron's guides serve as ample preparation for any high school history course.
  • Ethel Wood Books
    • Like books in the Barron's Test Preparation series, books written by Ethel Wood serve as excellent study guides for all AP history courses, including AP Human Geography, AP World History, and AP Government. English

High school english courses can be difficult for a lot of students due to the complexity of the passages being given and the idea of analsis versus summary as seen in advanced english courses. Use these courses to help understand what is needed to excel in high school english courses.

Common high school english courses in order:

Pre-AP/Pre-AP Quest/On Level English 1, Pre-AP/Pre-AP Quest/On Level English 2, On Level English 3/AP English Language and Composition, On Level English 4/AP English Literature, AP Seminar*, AP Research*

These courses aim to prepare high school students for college-level english. Use the following resources to help master the said courses.

  • Crash Course Literature Series
    • John Greene explains key rhetorical and analytical strategies needed to fully digest a novel or piece. These can be adapted to any high school english course.
  • AP Study Notes: AP English
    • AP Study Notes provides sample essays, rhetorical terms, and extra essay tips to help prepare for the AP English Language and Composition/AP Literature courses. The information found on this resource can be adapted to all high school english courses.
  • Thesaurus.com
    • Although not technically a resource, it always helps to keep this synonym search engine out while working on english essays and assignments to avoid simple words like "big." This resource can be used for any high school english course.
  • Transition Words
    • This site provides a wide vareity of transition words while writing for an english assignment or essay. Using transition words adds to the structure and flow of an essay.This resource can be used for any high school english course.
  • Syntax Terms
    • Syntax refers to the struture of a written piece, and how certain patterns or techniques add a deeper meaning to the writing. Use these syntax terms to become more familiar with how you can both recognize syntax terms in others' writing and provide a more complex meaning to your own writing. This becomes more useful in AP Language and Composition/On Level English 3 and beyond.
  • Tone words
    • The addition of tone words in any essay or assignment adds precision and illustration to the ideas expressed in your paper. Use this site for a broad range of verbs and nouns to polish your writing. This resource can be used for any high school english course.
* These courses may not follow the course order listed or may be optional.

College Planning

The college application process is stressful for every student because of how much needs to be accomplished. Seniors need to choose their major and the colleges they want to apply to for that major, take exams such as the SAT and ACT, write multiple essays for the schools they have chosen, and worry about the transition from high school to college. Here are some resources that will guide seniors through this tumultuous process.


SAT and ACT

The SAT and ACT are standardized exams that all high schoolers have to take in order to attend college. The Scholarly Aptitude Test, or SAT, tests on mathematics until Algebra 2 and some of Pre-Calculus, english comprehension, and writing skills. The American College Testing test, or ACT, tests on mathematics up to Pre-Calculus, english comprehension, writing skills, and scientific reasoning. High schoolers often study extensively but may not reap the benefits because they were not studying correctly. Use these resources to find practice tests and problem sets to narrow in on your weaknesses and attain a score that you will be comfortable with. You may need to use only one or a combination of these resources to reach your target score, based on your current level and your work ethic.

  • Khan Academy SAT Practice
    • Khan Academy's SAT Practice Program is partnered with Collegeboard to link with your past PSAT** and SAT scores to detect which types of problems you are scoring low on. The program then has a practice page with problems for all 4 sections: Reading, Writing, Math with no calculator, and Math with calculator. Each skill for a specific section is ranked from 1-4 indicating your mastery of a specific skill. The Khan Academy SAT program has proven to be one of the best SAT preparation resources for high schoolers, no matter your target score.
  • The Official SAT Study Guide
    • This study guide is published by Collegeboard and details everything you need to know about the SAT, from problem types to techniques and strategies to follow when taking the SAT. This book also includes the 8 official Collegeboard practice SAT tests in order to measure the effectiveness of your current SAT study plans.
  • Barron's SAT Practice Book
    • Like the Official SAT Study Guide, the Barron's SAT Practice Book walks through every possible problem type seen on the SAT and includes more tips and tricks on how to ace the SAT.
  • Erica Meltzer's Study Guides
    • These study guides expand on all the strategies needed not only for the Reading and Writing portions of the SAT but also for the English portions of the ACT.
  • PrepScholar
    • PrepScholar is a creator of online blogs and prepatory courses for both the ACT and SAT. Their online articles help to internalize some of the SAT and ACT strategies that may be difficult to grasp as they may not be covered heavily in high school classrooms, such as the importance of skimming passages. Their online SAT and ACT courses do cost, but they provide an exhaustive bank of practice problems that automatically pinpoint your weaknesses in order to make you as well-rounded as possible before the test.
  • UWorld SAT Course
    • The UWorld SAT Course is not free but offers a 30 day free trial for you to experiment with. Like Khan Academy, this course has a bank of around 250 Math problems, 500 Writing Problems (around 50 passages), and 730 Reading Problems (around 75 passsages). Evidently, the Uworld course is an excellent resource for problems and the explanations for answers makes the problem solving strategy for each SAT question crystal clear.
** Preliminary SAT. It's only an indicator of how well you will do on your actual SAT and does not play a role in the college admission process but can get you National Merit Commended/Semifinalist/Finalist scholarships.


General College Resources

The following resources will help you piece together the many components of a college application, from how you should go about choosing your major to help on essay writing.

  • Collegeboard
    • The Collegeboard website is a great place to start with college applications. It has custom college search engines to best fit your desires and has vast information on courses/tests you can take to get ready for college, like AP courses and the SAT. Additionally, the Collegeboard website can help with financial aid and scholarships, removing a lot of stress for college application in a lot of families.
  • Naviance
    • Naviance does a great job of consolidating academic information in preparation for college applications. It records your test scores, helps you create a custom resume, has surveys to help you determine what career you may desire, and stores resources that can help you search and apply for college for your chosen major. Using the resources on Naviance can remove the confusion when applying for college.
